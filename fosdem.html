Docs at FOSDEM

Every year, FOSDEM in Brussels brings together the free and open-source software community for two days of talks. In 2025 and 2026, Virgile Deville — Product Manager of Docs — went there to present the project: how it's built, who funds it, and how it collaborates with the libraries it depends on.

Below are recordings of every talk, straight from the FOSDEM video archive.

FOSDEM 2026

Three talks in Brussels

Docs was covered from three angles: the product itself, the libraries it's built on, and where it fits in the Local First movement.

FOSDEM 2026 Tool the Docs

LaSuite Docs: open source collaborative documentation platform

A tour of Docs as a block-based, open-source editor built by the French and German governments: real-time collaboration, granular permissions, sub-documents, and a headless CMS API — and why it suits teams looking to build, manage, and scale documentation.

Event page · Download video
FOSDEM 2026 Building Europe's Public Digital Infrastructure

Building digital workplace solutions on top of foundational libraries

With Yousef El-Dardiry. How France and Germany fund core features — exports, comments, edit attribution, suggestions — directly into BlockNote and Yjs rather than into Docs alone, why funding libraries differs from funding applications, and what the whole ecosystem gains from it.

Event page · Download video
FOSDEM 2026 Local-First, sync engines, CRDTs

A Local First collaborative workplace?

A lightning talk introducing LaSuite, DINUM's MIT-licensed suite built around Docs, Drive, Meet, and an AI assistant, and its reusable UI Kit — offered as a resource for the Local First community building its own collaborative apps.

Event page · Download video
FOSDEM 2025

Where the European collaboration started

FOSDEM 2025 Government Collaboration

Note-Worthy Collaboration: Co-developing a Note-Taking Application

With Alexander Smolianitski and Yousef El-Dardiry. The story of the 100-day challenge where French and German product teams joined forces to co-develop a note-taking application for their respective public-sector suites, La Suite Numérique and openDesk — design decisions, technical implementation, and lessons learned.
