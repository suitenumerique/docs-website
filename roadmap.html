How Docs gets built

Docs is an open-source project (the code is open and reusable) and a digital commons (a community contributes, and various stakeholders fund and decide on developments).

Today, it is mainly public institutions who participate:

  • DINUM 🇫🇷 — Interministerial Directorate for Digital Affairs (Direction Interministérielle du Numérique)
  • ZenDiS 🇩🇪 — Center for Digital Sovereignty (Zentrum für Digitale Souveränität)
  • MinBZK 🇳🇱 — Ministry of the Interior and Kingdom Relations (Ministerie van Binnenlandse Zaken en Koninkrijksrelaties)
  • EC 🇪🇺 — European Commission (Commission Européenne)

Docs is built on open-source libraries, some of which are listed as "dependencies" in the feature list below because that is where development is needed to offer them to Docs users.

Our most important dependencies

Name Origin Maturity Downloads / month Funded by Docs
Django Rest Framework 🇬🇧 UK Very mature — created in 2011, considered feature-complete by its maintainers; maintenance is focused on stability and Django/Python compatibility. ~28M No
Next.js 🇺🇸 US Mature — launched in 2016, maintained by Vercel, widely adopted in production, but evolves rapidly (structural changes between major versions). ~165M No
BlockNote.js 🇳🇱 Netherlands Young — recent project under active development, API still evolving, growing community. Maintained by a small team of 3 developers. ~1.5M Yes (🇫🇷 DINUM, 🇩🇪 ZenDiS, 🇪🇺 EC)
Yjs 🇩🇪 Germany Mature — the reference for CRDTs in collaborative editing, stable, production-tested by many products. Maintained by a single developer. ~25M Yes (🇫🇷 DINUM, 🇩🇪 ZenDiS, 🇪🇺 EC)
DocSpec 🇳🇱 Netherlands Emerging — very active development (v1.20.0 released late June 2026), but format coverage is still partial. Maintained by a single developer. N/A Yes (🇫🇷 DINUM, 🇪🇺 EC)

If some are very mature, others are still young or sometimes maintained by a single developer. We are proud to fund their work and encourage other organizations to do the same, to avoid the following situation:

It is possible and desirable to work directly with these maintainers. This benefits the entire commons ecosystem, because this is how we build commons (WeMakeCommons).

We presented a playbook on how to do this well at FOSDEM — you can watch the conference video or read the playbook itself. Feel free to draw inspiration from it, and share your feedback. See all our FOSDEM talks for the rest of what we presented in 2025 and 2026.
